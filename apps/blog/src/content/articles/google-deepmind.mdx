## Summary

Google's AI research lab. In this corpus it appears as the lab behind [AI-Driven Formal Proof Search](/articles/ai-driven-formal-proof-search) — the team (George Tsoukalas, Anton Kovsharov, Sergey Shirobokov, Swarat Chaudhuri, Pushmeet Kohli et al.) that built [AlphaProof Nexus](/articles/alphaproof-nexus) and ran the first large-scale evaluation of LLM-aided formal proof search on open research mathematics (arXiv 2605.22763). It is also the maker of the **Gemini** model family used throughout (Gemini 3.1 Pro as prover, Gemini 3.0 Flash as rater), the prior **AlphaProof** olympiad theorem-prover, and **AlphaEvolve**, whose evolutionary design inspired [Evolutionary Proof Search](/articles/evolutionary-proof-search).

## Role in the corpus

DeepMind is the third frontier-lab "voice" in the wiki alongside [Anthropic](/articles/anthropic) and OpenAI ([Symphony](/articles/symphony) / [Agent Harness Engineering](/articles/agent-harness-engineering)), and the one that opens the **AI-for-mathematics** domain. Its contribution is methodological as much as mathematical: the paper's finding that simple agentic loops increasingly rival DeepMind's *own* bespoke trained systems ([Agentic Loops Overtake Bespoke Systems](/articles/agentic-loops-overtake-bespoke-systems)) is a candid, self-undercutting result — a lab that built specialized RL provers reporting that a plain LLM loop is catching up.

It is also the source of the wiki's **theory-of-superintelligence** cluster. The June 2026 report *[From AGI to ASI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12683)* — senior-authored by co-founder [Shane Legg](/articles/shane-legg) with [Marcus Hutter](/articles/marcus-hutter) (creator of [AIXI](/articles/universal-ai-aixi)) and twelve others — maps the [four pathways from AGI to ASI](/articles/agi-to-asi-pathways), grounds them in the [Universal AI](/articles/universal-ai-aixi) upper bound, and frames the frictions (the [The Abstraction Barrier](/articles/abstraction-barrier), the data wall, deliberate slowdown) as open research questions. Where [Anthropic's](/articles/anthropic) *When AI builds itself* argues [RSI](/articles/recursive-self-improvement) from internal measurement, DeepMind's report is the **theory-first sibling** — same question, formal framing.

## Systems and models referenced

- **Gemini 3.1 Pro / 3.0 Flash / 3.1 Flash-Lite** — the LLM backbone; Pro for proving, Flash for rating; the smaller variants solved no problems (capability is sharply scale-gated — [Scale-Dependent Prompt Sensitivity](/articles/scale-dependent-prompt-sensitivity)).
- **AlphaProof** — DeepMind's RL-trained olympiad-level Lean prover; used inside Nexus as a focused subgoal tool (and the system behind earlier IMO results).
- **AlphaEvolve** — the evolutionary-coding system whose population/diversity approach [Evolutionary Proof Search](/articles/evolutionary-proof-search) adapts; also helped formulate the bipartite graph-reconstruction variants in the paper.
- **Formal Conjectures repo** — DeepMind's open-source Lean formalizations of Erdős problems, the benchmark for the Erdős runs.
- **AutoRaters** — the adaptive [LLM-as-a-Judge](/articles/llm-as-a-judge) graders at the core of Google Cloud's [Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform](/articles/gemini-enterprise-agent-platform) evaluation service, developed in close partnership with DeepMind and (per Google) the same ones used to evaluate its own models and first-party agents; the grading engine of the [Agent Quality Flywheel](/articles/agent-quality-flywheel).
