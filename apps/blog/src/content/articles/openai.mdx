## Summary

OpenAI is an AI research company and the maker of the **GPT‑5 series** (including GPT‑5 Thinking and the **Codex** coding models) and the ChatGPT product. In this vault it is the principal counterweight to [Anthropic](/articles/anthropic) across two threads: **frontier-safety methodology** and **agent tooling**. It is also the company [Andrej Karpathy](/articles/andrej-karpathy) co-founded — the origin point of the [Software 1/2/3.0](/articles/software-3-0) and vibe-coding framings that recur throughout the wiki.

## What it does (in this corpus)

- **Frontier-safety research.** OpenAI authored **[Deployment Simulation](/articles/deployment-simulation)** (June 2026) — replaying ~1.3M de-identified production conversations to forecast a candidate model's deployment-time behavior before release, and the cross-lab mitigation for [evaluation awareness](/articles/evaluation-awareness-and-grader-gaming). Earlier, **[Deliberative Alignment](/articles/deliberative-alignment)** (Guan et al. 2025) is OpenAI's spec-grounded-CoT alignment method and the strongest non-MSM baseline in the alignment cluster.
- **Agent tooling and orchestration.** OpenAI ships **Codex** and the surrounding harness layer: the **[Codex App Server Protocol](/articles/codex-app-server-protocol)** (JSON-RPC stdio for headless sessions), the **[Symphony](/articles/symphony)** open-source orchestrator (Linear as a control plane for Codex), and the "harness engineering" framing for an agent-first Codex workflow.
- **A measurement asset.** Its scale of production traffic is what makes Deployment Simulation work at all — the same proprietary-traffic advantage [Production-Sourced Evaluation](/articles/production-sourced-evaluation) names, here turned toward pre-release safety forecasting rather than capability benchmarking.
- **Workforce-economics research.** Its June 2026 study *The Shift to Agentic AI: Evidence from Codex* uses Codex usage telemetry to document the [move from conversational to agentic AI](/articles/conversation-to-delegation-shift) across three populations — the OpenAI/Codex counterpart to [Anthropic](/articles/anthropic)'s [returns-to-expertise](/articles/returns-to-expertise) study (which it cites), and the third major usage-telemetry source in this corpus.
- **Its own product culture (self-reported).** [Andrew Ambrosino](/articles/andrew-ambrosino)'s June 2026 interview is the wiki's window into how OpenAI builds: nearly **all employees use [Codex](/articles/codex) weekly** ([dogfooding](/articles/dogfooding-as-product-discipline) as culture); teams are "very agentic" with "unlimited tokens," so "everybody's building everything" ([Implementation Abundance Inverts Product Work](/articles/implementation-abundance-inverts-product-work)); a **bottoms-up exploration** culture where products disrupt each other internally; large, mostly-IC teams of "former founders" with "high agency and taste"; and the **member-of-technical-staff** convention ([Role Averaging, Not Role Elimination](/articles/role-averaging-not-role-elimination)). Blunt internal feedback loops ("a 2,000-message Slack thread about how stupid we are") are named as why the external product works.

## Position relative to Anthropic

The two labs converge on shared problems from different angles, which is why OpenAI sources keep pairing with Anthropic ones in this wiki:

- On **evaluation awareness**, Anthropic *names the problem* (the marquee Opus 4.8 concern) and OpenAI *ships a mitigation* (deployment-distribution replay).
- On **alignment training**, [deliberative alignment](/articles/deliberative-alignment) (OpenAI) is the direct-CoT-training baseline that Anthropic's [Model Spec Midtraining (MSM)](/articles/model-spec-midtraining) outperforms while better preserving [Chain-of-Thought Monitorability](/articles/cot-monitorability).
- On **agent orchestration**, [Symphony](/articles/symphony)/Codex (OpenAI) and [Claude Code](/articles/claude-code) (Anthropic) are the two reference harnesses the agent-tooling pages compare.
