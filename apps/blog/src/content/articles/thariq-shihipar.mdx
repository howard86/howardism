## Summary

Engineer on the [Claude Code](/articles/claude-code) team at [Anthropic](/articles/anthropic). Source of the **"HTML is the new markdown"** thesis (see [HTML as the New Markdown](/articles/html-as-the-new-markdown)) and the **[Compute Allocator](/articles/compute-allocator)** framing of the human role. Interviewed by [Claire Vo](/articles/claire-vo) for the "How I AI" series after the two met at Code with Claude in San Francisco (episode published May 2026). Personal site thariq.io; on X as @trq212.

## Notable claims and stances

- **"HTML is the new markdown."** As models get more capable and projects more complex, markdown plans overwhelm the human reader; HTML is a richer medium that keeps humans engaged. The model isn't the bottleneck — human attention is. See [HTML as the New Markdown](/articles/html-as-the-new-markdown).
- **We're all becoming "compute allocators."** The human's job is deciding what's worth spending compute on; ~1% of his generated tokens reach production, the other 99% is scaffolding (plans, interfaces, status, design systems) invested in alignment and communication. See [Compute Allocator](/articles/compute-allocator).
- **Abundance mindset.** Generation is cheap enough to afford throwaway tooling — [micro-apps](/articles/disposable-micro-apps) built for a single editing task ("micro-software on top of micro-software").
- **Constraint plus latitude prompting.** "Provide enough constraint to get what you want, but leave room for the model to surprise you" — *"Hey, Claude, I trust you here."*
- **Recenter the human.** His workflows are explicitly *not* about automating the developer away; they make collaboration more visible and engaging.
- **The map is not the territory.** (July 2026 field guide.) Your prompts, skills, and context are the map; the codebase and its real constraints are the territory; the gap is *unknowns*. **"Fable is the first model where I find the quality of the work is bottlenecked by my ability to clarify its unknowns."** Reducing and planning for unknowns "is the **skill** of agentic coding" — and it is learnable. See [Unknowns as the Agentic Bottleneck](/articles/unknowns-as-the-agentic-bottleneck).
- **The quiz gate.** "I only merge after I pass the quiz perfectly" — he has Claude quiz him on a change before merging, because reading diffs under-informs when behavior depends on existing code paths. The human, not the agent, is what gets verified.
- **Source code is the best reference.** When you can't describe what you want, point at code that does it — even in another language. This is how [Claude Design](/articles/claude-design) works: point it at a component and "it reads the underlying code, not just the screenshot."

## The three workflows he demonstrated

1. **[Interactive HTML brainstorming and planning](/articles/html-as-the-new-markdown)** — ask for plans as HTML files, not lists; visual, scrollable, shareable.
2. **[Disposable Micro-Apps](/articles/disposable-micro-apps)** — generate a throwaway custom UI to edit one part of a plan, with copy-back-to-markdown.
3. **[Living Design System](/articles/living-design-system)** — a `design_system.html` extracted from repos as a portable, human- and machine-readable source of truth.
