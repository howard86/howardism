## What it is

An AI research lab (publishes under "Thinking Machines Lab: Connectionism"). In this wiki it first appears as the org behind **<Link href="/articles/interaction-models">Interaction Models</Link>** — a May 2026 research preview reframing real-time human-AI collaboration as a model-native capability rather than a harness concern.

## What they've shipped / argued (as seen here)

- **<Link href="/articles/interaction-models">Interaction Models</Link>** (May 2026 research preview) — models that natively take in audio/video/text and think/respond/act in real time. First model: **<Link href="/articles/tml-interaction-small">TML-Interaction-Small</Link>** (276B MoE, 12B active).
- Position: **interactivity should scale with intelligence** → it must be in the model, citing <Link href="/articles/the-bitter-lesson">The Bitter Lesson</Link> against harness-based real-time systems (VAD, turn-detection).
- Engineering footprint: upstreamed a streaming-sessions feature to **SGLang**; published work on [defeating nondeterminism in LLM inference](https://thinkingmachines.ai/blog/defeating-nondeterminism-in-llm-inference/) (batch-invariant kernels), referenced for trainer-sampler alignment; prior post **On-Policy Distillation**.
- Running a **research grant** for interactivity / human-AI-collaboration benchmarks (details TBA); limited research preview of the interaction model "in the coming months," wider release "later this year"; larger models promised later in 2026.

## How it connects

- Stakes out a different priority than the labs critiqued in <Link href="/articles/turn-based-interface-bottleneck">Turn-Based Interface Bottleneck</Link> ("AI labs over-optimize for autonomy") — implicitly positioning against the autonomy-first framing seen around <Link href="/articles/anthropic">Anthropic</Link>'s and OpenAI's agent products (<Link href="/articles/claude-code">Claude Code</Link>, <Link href="/articles/symphony">Symphony</Link>).
- Their harness-dissolves-into-model stance is the same shape as <Link href="/articles/harness-shrinkage-as-models-improve">Harness Shrinkage as Models Improve</Link> (an <Link href="/articles/anthropic">Anthropic</Link>/<Link href="/articles/claude-code">Claude Code</Link> observation) — convergent thinking from different labs.
- Benchmarks their model against **GPT-realtime-2.0 / 1.5** (OpenAI) and **Gemini-3.1-flash-live** (Google) and **Qwen 3.5 Omni** — see <Link href="/articles/interactivity-benchmarks">Interactivity Benchmarks</Link>.
